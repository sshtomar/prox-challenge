# Page 8: Front Panel Controls Diagram

A labeled photograph of the front of the Vulcan OmniPro 220 welder with callout arrows identifying every control and connector.

## Layout (top to bottom, left to right):

### Top Row (Control Panel Area):
- **Home Button** -- upper-left corner of the control panel, labeled "HOME". Used to return to the main menu screen.
- **Back Button** -- upper-right corner of the control panel, labeled "BACK". Used to return to previous screen.
- **LCD Display** -- large rectangular screen in the center of the control panel. Shows welding process selection, settings, and status information.
- **Control Knob** (also called Main Control Knob) -- large central rotary knob below the LCD display, between the Home and Back buttons. Used to scroll through menus and select options. Can also be pressed to confirm selections.
- **Left Knob** -- smaller rotary knob to the left of the Main Control Knob, marked with amperage (A) symbol. Used to adjust amperage/wire feed speed depending on the process selected.
- **Right Knob** -- smaller rotary knob to the right of the Main Control Knob, marked with voltage (V) symbol. Used to adjust voltage or to turn the output ON/OFF.

### Middle Row (Left Side):
- **MIG Gun / Spool Gun Cable Socket** -- round connector on the left side of the front panel. The MIG gun cable plugs into this socket.
- **Spool Gun Gas Outlet** -- small fitting below the MIG Gun socket, on the left side. Used to connect gas hose when using an optional Spool Gun (sold separately).

### Middle Row (Right Side):
- **Storage Compartment** -- rectangular compartment door on the right side of the front panel. Used to store accessories.

### Bottom Row (Connector Panel):
From left to right along the bottom of the welder front:
- **Spool Gun Gas Outlet** (additional reference)
- **Negative (-) Socket** -- large round twist-lock connector, marked with a minus (-) sign. Cables twist clockwise to lock.
- **Wire Feed Power Cable** -- thick cable that exits the welder between the negative and positive sockets. This cable carries power from the welder to the wire feed mechanism and out through the MIG gun.
- **Positive (+) Socket** -- large round twist-lock connector on the far right, marked with a plus (+) sign. Cables twist clockwise to lock.

### Critical Note:
The Negative and Positive sockets are where the Ground Clamp cable and the Wire Feed Power Cable (or Electrode Holder cable for Stick, or TIG Torch cable for TIG) connect. Which cable goes in which socket depends on the welding process and required polarity (DCEP or DCEN). This is detailed on pages 13-14 (MIG/Flux), 24 (TIG), and 27 (Stick).

**Note:** When using an optional Spool Gun (sold separately), connect the Spool Gun gas hose to the Spool Gun Gas Outlet.
