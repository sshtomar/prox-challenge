# Selection Chart: How to Choose a Welder / Welding Process

A large color-coded decision matrix comparing all four welding processes across six criteria. The chart is organized as a grid with four columns (one per process) and six rows (one per criterion), plus a summary recommendation row at the bottom.

## Column Headers (Left to Right):
1. **FLUX-CORED / FCAW** -- orange/red header
2. **MIG / GMAW** -- orange/red header
3. **STICK / SMAW** -- orange/red header
4. **TIG / GTAW** -- orange/red header

## Row 1: What Is Your Skill Level?
| Process | Skill Level |
|---|---|
| Flux-Cored | **LOW** to Moderate to High (LOW highlighted in green) |
| MIG | LOW to **MODERATE** to High (MODERATE highlighted in orange) |
| Stick | LOW to Moderate to **HIGH** (unlabeled, spread across) |
| TIG | LOW to Moderate to **HIGH** (HIGH highlighted in red) |

## Row 2: Will You Need Shielding Gas?
| Process | Gas Requirement |
|---|---|
| Flux-Cored | **NO GAS REQUIRED** |
| MIG | **GAS REQUIRED** (Indoor welding recommended) |
| Stick | **NO GAS REQUIRED** |
| TIG | **GAS REQUIRED** (Indoor welding recommended) |

## Row 3: What Type of Material Will You Be Welding?
| Process | Materials |
|---|---|
| Flux-Cored | **Steel, Stainless Steel** |
| MIG | **Steel, Stainless Steel, Aluminum** (Spool gun required for aluminum) |
| Stick | **Steel, Stainless Steel, Castings** |
| TIG | **Steel, Stainless Steel, Chrome Moly** (DC TIG required), **Aluminum, Magnesium Alloys** (AC TIG required) |

## Row 4: What Is Your Material Thickness?
| Process | Thickness Range |
|---|---|
| Flux-Cored | **18 Gauge to 5/16"** |
| MIG | **22 Gauge to 3/8"** |
| Stick | **10 Gauge to 1/2"** |
| TIG | **24 Gauge to 3/16"** |

## Row 5: Typical Applications
| Process | Applications |
|---|---|
| Flux-Cored | Galvanized steel, General fabrication, Pipe and tubing, Maintenance and repair |
| MIG | Sheet metal, Automotive body, Tubing, Structural steel |
| Stick | Pipe and tubing, Structural steel, Pressure vessels, Maintenance and repair |
| TIG | Stainless steel exhausts, Bicycle frames, Thin wall pipe and tubing, Metal art |

## Row 6: How Clean Do You Need Your Weld?
| Process | Cleanliness |
|---|---|
| Flux-Cored | **MORE SPATTER** |
| MIG | **CLEAN / MINIMAL SPATTER** |
| Stick | **MORE SPATTER** |
| TIG | **EXTREMELY CLEAN** |

## Bottom Summary Row (Process Recommendations):

### FLUX-CORED / FCAW:
- Ideal for outdoor or windy conditions
- Forgiving on rusty or dirty steels
- Good out of position welding capabilities
- High deposition rates achievable

### MIG / GMAW:
- Fast production (high welding speeds)
- Easiest to learn
- Clean welds with no slag
- Better control on thin materials

### STICK / SMAW:
- Ideal for outdoor or windy conditions
- Forgiving on rusty or dirty steels
- Deep penetration
- Good choice for thicker materials

### TIG / GTAW:
- Highest quality welds
- Extremely aesthetic weld appearance
- Can be used on a variety of materials
- Precise control
