# Page 35: Wire Weld Diagnosis -- Heat Control and Weld Penetration

This is one of the most critical visual pages in the manual. It provides visual comparison guides for diagnosing wire weld quality.

## Upper Section: Workpiece Heat Control / Weld Penetration

Three cross-section diagrams showing weld penetration into a butt joint:

### 1. INADEQUATE PENETRATION (Left)
- Cross-section shows two metal pieces with a weld bead on top that barely penetrates into the joint
- The weld sits mostly on the surface, with little fusion into the base metal
- Label: **"Not hot enough"**
- **How to increase workpiece heat and increase penetration (to weld THICKER workpieces properly):**
  - a. Increase weld current
  - b. Decrease travel speed
  - c. Use faster wire feed
  - d. Use shorter CTWD (Contact Tip to Work Distance)

### 2. PROPER PENETRATION (Center)
- Cross-section shows the weld bead fully fused into the joint with good penetration through the full thickness
- The weld profile is smooth and even, with the root fully fused
- Label: **"Ideal heat"**

### 3. EXCESS PENETRATION OR BURN-THROUGH (Right)
- Cross-section shows the weld has melted completely through the base metal, with material drooping through the bottom
- Excessive hole or bulge on the underside
- Label: **"Too hot"**
- **How to reduce workpiece heat and limit penetration (to weld THINNER workpieces properly):**
  - e. Decrease weld current
  - f. Increase travel speed
  - g. Use slower wire feed
  - h. Use longer CTWD

## Lower Section: Example Wire Weld Diagrams

Six side-by-side photographs/illustrations of actual weld beads, each showing a different condition. Each has a top-down view of the weld bead surface AND a cross-section view showing penetration.

### 1. Good Weld (Far Left)
- **Top view**: Smooth, consistent bead with even ripple pattern
- **Cross section**: Even penetration, smooth profile
- No correction needed

### 2. Voltage Too Low or Wire Feed Too Slow
- **Top view**: Tall, narrow, rope-like bead with excessive buildup
- **Cross section**: Poor fusion to sides of joint, tall narrow profile
- **TO CORRECT**: Increase Output Voltage OR Increase Wire Feed Speed

### 3. Voltage Too High or Wire Feed Too Fast
- **Top view**: Flat, wide bead with excessive spatter (dots scattered around the bead)
- **Cross section**: Wide, flat profile with spatter
- **TO CORRECT**: Decrease Output Voltage OR Decrease Wire Feed Speed

### 4. Travel Speed Too Fast
- **Top view**: Thin, narrow, inconsistent bead -- looks stretched
- **Cross section**: Narrow with inadequate fill
- **TO CORRECT**: Travel slower (illustration shows a hand slowing down travel direction)

### 5. Travel Speed Too Slow
- **Top view**: Very wide, excessive buildup, lumpy bead
- **Cross section**: Overfilled with excessive width
- **TO CORRECT**: Travel faster (illustration shows a hand speeding up travel direction)

### 6. CTWD Too Long or Wrong Polarity (Far Right)
- **Top view**: Irregular bead with heavy spatter (many dots/balls scattered around)
- **Cross section**: Poor penetration, excessive spatter
- **TO CORRECT**: Check Polarity AND maintain less than 1/2" CTWD

## Visual Diagnosis Quick Reference:
| Symptom | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Tall, narrow, ropy bead | Voltage too low / wire feed too slow | Increase voltage or wire speed |
| Flat, wide bead with spatter | Voltage too high / wire feed too fast | Decrease voltage or wire speed |
| Thin, stretched bead | Travel speed too fast | Slow down |
| Wide, lumpy, overfilled bead | Travel speed too slow | Speed up |
| Irregular bead, heavy spatter | CTWD too long or wrong polarity | Check polarity, shorten CTWD to under 1/2" |
