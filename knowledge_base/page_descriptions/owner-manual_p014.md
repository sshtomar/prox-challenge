# Page 14: DCEP Polarity Setup + Shielding Gas Connection

This page covers DCEP (Direct Current Electrode Positive) polarity setup for solid core gas-shielded MIG welding, and the full shielding gas cylinder connection procedure.

## Upper Section: DCEP Polarity Setup (Step 17)

### Title: "DCEP Solid Core (Gas Shielded) Polarity Setup"

Large diagram showing the bottom front of the welder with cable routing -- the REVERSE of the DCEN setup on page 13:

- **Ground Clamp Cable** connects to the **Negative (-) Socket** (left socket as viewed from front)
- **Wire Feed Power Cable** connects to the **Positive (+) Socket** (right socket as viewed from front)

Labels: "DCEP", "Ground Clamp Cable in Negative Socket", "Wire Feed Power Cable in Positive Socket"

Both cables twist clockwise all the way to lock in place.

## POLARITY COMPARISON (pages 13 vs 14):
| Cable | DCEN (Flux-Cored/Gasless) p.13 | DCEP (Solid Core/Gas Shielded) p.14 |
|---|---|---|
| Wire Feed Power Cable | Negative (-) Socket | Positive (+) Socket |
| Ground Clamp Cable | Positive (+) Socket | Negative (-) Socket |

## Lower Section: Shielding Gas Connection (Steps 17b through 17f)

Sequential diagram showing 6 steps to connect a shielding gas cylinder:

### Step b: Determine shielding gas type
Refer to the Settings Chart on the inside of the Welder door.

### Step c: Position the gas cylinder
Diagram shows an upright gas cylinder being secured to a cabinet or cart near the welder with two straps to prevent tipping. Two straps wrap around the cylinder at different heights.

### Step d: Clean the valve
Diagram shows hands opening the cylinder valve briefly to blow out dust and dirt, then closing it. Arrow and text: "Briefly open valve to clean, then close valve."

### Step e: Attach the regulator
Diagram shows the regulator being threaded onto the cylinder valve and wrench-tightened. The regulator's inlet connects to the cylinder valve.

**Note:** When using C100 shielding gas, connect the enclosed CGA 580/320 adapter to the inlet connection of the Regulator and wrench-tighten. Thread the adapter onto the gas cylinder and wrench-tighten.

### Step f: Connect the gas hose
Diagram shows the gas hose being attached from the regulator's outlet to the welder's gas inlet. Both connections must be wrench-tightened.

The overall flow shown in the diagram sequence: Cylinder -> Regulator -> Gas Hose -> Welder gas inlet.
