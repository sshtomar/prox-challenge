# Quick Start Guide - Page 1: MIG/Flux-Cored Wire Setup

A single-page visual guide with 6 numbered steps for setting up the welder for wire (MIG or Flux-Cored) welding. Each step has a numbered orange circle, brief text instruction, and one or more photographs.

## Left Side: Wire Spool Loading Reference
Labeled diagram of the wire spool assembly:
- **Wingnut** -- secures the spool to the spindle (top)
- **Spacer** -- sits between wingnut and spool
- **Spindle** -- the central shaft
- **2 lb Wire Spool** -- small spool shown mounted
- **Brake Pad** -- friction pad the spool rests against
- **Welder** wall shown behind the assembly
- Note at bottom: "Adapter and knob required for 10-12 lb spool"

## Step 1: Connect Cables
"Connect cables as needed for flux wire welding - see back of guide."
This refers to the polarity setup on page 2 of the quick start guide.

## Step 2: Install Wire Spool
"Install wire spool. Insert wire through both feed guides and clamp it into feed mechanism. See manual if changing wire size or using solid wire."
- Photo shows the **Feed Mechanism** area with labeled parts:
  - **feed guides** -- the tubes that guide wire from spool to feed rollers
  - **welding wire** -- the wire being threaded through
  - **Wire Spool** -- mounted on the spindle

## Step 3: Feed Wire Through
"Take off nozzle and tip, plug selected cord into welder and into wall, turn welder on, and press cold wire feed switch until wire comes out."
- Left diagram shows the MIG gun tip with labeled parts:
  - **Nozzle** -- outer cup, pull to remove
  - **Contact Tip** -- inner tip, 2" from nozzle end
- Right photo shows a hand holding the MIG gun with wire protruding

**WARNING: To prevent serious injury, read manual warnings and instructions before use.**
**WARNING: To prevent fire and serious injury, keep torch and wire clear of grounded objects while welder plugged in.**

## Step 4: Adjust Wire Feed Tension
"Incrementally tighten wire feed tension knob clockwise until wire will bend from feed tension at 2\"-3\"."
- Left diagram shows a hand turning the tension knob **clockwise** (arrow indicates direction)
- Right photo shows the wire being tested: it should bend under its own weight at a distance of **2\"-3\"** from where it exits the gun nozzle. A piece of wood (wood block) is shown as reference.

## Step 5: Replace Nozzle and Tip
"Unplug welder and replace nozzle and tip."
- Diagram shows nozzle and contact tip being reinstalled on the MIG gun, with an arrow indicating the assembly sequence.

## Step 6: Power On and Configure
"Hold gun clear of workpiece, then plug in welder, turn it on, and set weld thickness and other settings."
- Sequence of images showing:
  1. "HOLD GUN CLEAR" -- hand holding gun away from any metal
  2. Plugging the power cord into the wall outlet
  3. Turning the power switch ON
  4. The welder's LCD display showing the settings screen
- The welder front panel is visible with the LCD display active

**"BASIC WELDING INSTRUCTIONS AND WELDING TIPS ARE IN MANUAL."**
