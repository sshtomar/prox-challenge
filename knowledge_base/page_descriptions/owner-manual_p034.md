# Page 34: Strike Test + Weld Cleaning

## Strike Test Procedure

Visual guide for testing weld quality using a destructive bend test.

### Test Setup Diagram (Upper):
- Two welded scrap pieces clamped in a sturdy vise
- One scrap piece extends horizontally from the vise
- A **dead-blow hammer** is used to strike the extending piece downward
- Text: "Stay clear from underneath while you strike"

### Good Weld Result (Right-Upper):
- Diagram shows the weld bending/deforming but NOT breaking
- The weld joint remains intact, metal deforms around it
- Label: **"GOOD WELD bends and is not brittle"**
- Arrow shows the deformed metal bending at the weld joint

### Poor Weld Result (Right-Lower):
- Diagram shows the weld snapping/cracking at the joint
- The welded piece separates at the weld line
- Label: **"POOR WELD snaps or cracks"**
- Shows broken pieces separated at the joint

### Test Procedure:
1. After two scraps have been welded together and the weld has cooled, clamp one scrap in a sturdy vise
2. Stay clear from underneath while you strike the opposite scrap with a heavy hammer, preferably a dead-blow hammer
3. A GOOD WELD will deform but not break
4. A POOR WELD will be brittle and snap at the weld

**CAUTION! This test WILL damage the weld it is performed on. This test is ONLY an indicator of weld technique and is not intended to test working welds.**

## Cleaning the Weld (Lower Section)

### Tools Shown:
- **Chipping Hammer** -- pointed/flat tool for knocking off slag. Diagram shows it being used to chip slag from a weld bead.
- **Wire Brush** -- brush for further cleaning after chipping. Shown next to the chipping hammer.

### Cleaning Steps:
1. A weld from flux-cored wire welding or stick welding will be covered by slag. Use a chipping hammer to knock this off. **Be careful not to damage the weld or base material.**
2. Use a wire brush to further clean the weld, or use an angle grinder (sold separately) to shape the weld.

**WARNING: Continue to wear ANSI-approved safety goggles and protective wear when cleaning a weld. Sparks or chips may fly when cleaning.**
