# Page 19: MIG Duty Cycle Charts + Welding Technique

## MIG Rated Duty Cycles (Clock Diagrams)

Four clock-face diagrams showing the welding/resting time split for a 10-minute continuous period:

### 240VAC Duty Cycles:
1. **25% Use at 200A** -- Clock shows: 2.5 minutes welding (shaded), 7.5 minutes resting (unshaded). "For 10 Continuous Minutes"
2. **100% Continuous Use at 115A** -- Full clock shaded. Can weld continuously without resting at or below 115A on 240V.

### 120VAC Duty Cycles:
3. **40% Use at 100A** -- Clock shows: 4 minutes welding, 6 minutes resting. "For 10 Continuous Minutes"
4. **100% Continuous Use at 75A** -- Full clock shaded. Can weld continuously without resting at or below 75A on 120V.

## Duty Cycle Explanation Text:
- The Duty Cycle defines the number of minutes, within a 10 minute period, during which a given welder can produce a particular welding current without overheating.
- Example: a welder with a 40% duty cycle at 125A must rest at least 6 minutes after every 4 minutes of continuous welding.
- Failure to observe duty cycle limitations can over-stress the welder's power generation system.
- The welder has an internal thermal protection system that automatically shuts down and displays a warning on the LCD if overheated.
- Allow the welder to cool with the Power Switch on so the internal fan helps cool it.
