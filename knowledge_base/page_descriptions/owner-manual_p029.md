# Page 29: TIG and Stick Duty Cycle Charts

## TIG Rated Duty Cycles (Clock Diagrams)

### 240VAC:
- **30% Use at 175A** -- Clock shows 3 minutes welding, 7 minutes resting per 10 continuous minutes
- **100% Continuous Use at 105A** -- Full clock shaded, can weld continuously at or below 105A

### 120VAC:
- **40% Use at 125A** -- Clock shows 4 minutes welding, 6 minutes resting per 10 continuous minutes
- **100% Continuous Use at 90A** -- Full clock shaded, can weld continuously at or below 90A

## Stick Rated Duty Cycles (Clock Diagrams)

- **100% Continuous Use at 100A** -- Full clock shaded, can weld continuously at or below 100A

### 120VAC:
- **40% Use at 80A** -- Clock shows 4 minutes welding, 6 minutes resting per 10 continuous minutes
- **100% Continuous Use at 60A** -- Full clock shaded, can weld continuously at or below 60A

## Setting up the Weld (Lower Section)

Same workpiece preparation content as page 19, applicable to TIG/Stick:
- Clean surfaces to bare metal
- Chamfer thick workpieces for proper penetration
- Use clamps to hold workpieces in position
- Clamp Ground Cable to bare metal on workpiece near weld area

**NOTICE:** When welding equipment on a vehicle, disconnect the vehicle battery power from both the positive connection and the ground before welding. This prevents damage to vehicle electrical systems and electronics.
