# Page 26: Tungsten Electrode Sharpening + TIG Torch Assembly

## Upper Section: Sharpening Tungsten Electrode

### Grinding Diagram:
Shows the proper technique for sharpening a tungsten electrode on a grinding wheel:
- **Electrode** -- held at an angle against the **Grinding Wheel**
- The electrode tip is pressed lightly against the wheel surface and rotated to form a blunt point
- **"Grinding direction must be parallel to length of Electrode"** -- arrow showing the grinding direction runs along the length, not across it
- Two cross-section views show:
  - Correct: conical tip formed by parallel grinding (checkmark)
  - Incorrect: tip formed by perpendicular grinding (X mark)
- The conical portion of the ideal tip will be **2-1/2 times as long as the Electrode diameter**

### Sharpening Procedure:
1. Shut off the welder and wait until Electrode and Torch have cooled
2. Remove Back Cap, pull Electrode from front of Torch
3. If Electrode is dulled or contaminated, snap off the contaminated section
4. Press tip against grinding wheel at an angle, rotate until blunt point is formed
5. Conical tip should be 2.5x the electrode diameter in length
6. Re-insert into Collet with tip protruding 1/8" to 1/4" beyond Ceramic Nozzle

## Lower Section: TIG Torch Assembly (Exploded Diagram)

Labeled exploded view of the TIG Torch showing all components in assembly order:

### From front (welding end) to back:
1. **Ceramic Nozzle** -- threaded ceramic cup at the front. Must be appropriate size for application. Threads onto the Collet Body.
2. **Collet Body** -- threaded into the front of the Torch body. Holds the Collet and Nozzle.
3. **Collet** -- split copper tube that grips the tungsten electrode. Insert into back of Torch and into Collet Body. Must match Tungsten Electrode size.
4. **Tungsten Electrode** -- inserted through the back of the Torch, through the Collet, and protrudes out the front. Arrow labeled "Insert tungsten Electrode here" shows insertion from the back.
5. **Back Cap** -- threaded cap on the rear of the Torch. Locks the Electrode in place by compressing the Collet. Tighten to secure electrode at proper protrusion (1/8" to 1/4" beyond Ceramic Nozzle).

### Assembly Steps:
1. Thread Collet Body into the front of the Torch
2. Make sure Ceramic Nozzle size is appropriate for application
3. Thread Ceramic Nozzle onto Collet Body
4. Insert Collet into back of Torch and into Collet Body
5. Insert Tungsten Electrode into back of Torch
6. Lock Electrode in place with Back Cap. Electrode should protrude 1/8" to 1/4" beyond the Ceramic Nozzle.

### Key Sizing Rule:
Match Collet and Collet Body sizes to Tungsten Electrode size. All three must be the same diameter.
